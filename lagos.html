Anvi Lagos — 01

Refined Indo-Asian, made for sharing.

A curated sharing menu that travels from North Indian clay-oven kebabs and slow-built curries to dim sum, wok and Indo-Chinese classics.

Plates arrive to be passed, torn and shared. Downstairs is warm, elegant and all-ages; upstairs the lights drop, the bar takes over, and two private rooms close their doors on the city. Drinks are mixed at the first-floor bar and find their way to every table in the house.

Ground floor · all ages

The Dining Room

Seventy-two covers of booths and family tables under warm, adjustable light — marble underfoot, brass inlays at the door, and a room that hums rather than roars. Elegant, but never precious: this is the floor for long dinners, birthdays that start early, and children who fall asleep in the booth.

The kitchen runs an open pass, so the tandoor's glow is part of the room.

First floor · 18+

The Lounge & Bar

A central bar in polished stone and brushed metal, shelves backlit like a jewellery case, and lounge seating that rewards staying put. This is where the signature cocktails are built — Asian and Indian flavours through a classic lens — and where the evening slows down on purpose.

Curated lounge music, low light, and service that notices your glass before you do.

First floor · private dining

Two rooms with no audience.

Two private dining rooms — twenty covers and twenty-two — behind soundproof, collapsible walls. Take one for a board dinner or a family celebration; fold the wall away and seat forty-two for the night you don't want to end.

The kitchen

Four stations, one pass.

A 650-square-foot kitchen built around four disciplines, each with its own chef and its own temper.

01

The Tandoor

Clay-oven heat for kebabs, tikkas and breads blistered to order.

02

The Curry Pot

Slow-built gravies — malai, makhani, korma — finished with patience.

03

The Steamer

Dim sum folded by hand — har gau, shumai, gyoza and bao.

04

The Wok

High-flame Indo-Chinese and Pan-Asian mains, seared in seconds.
